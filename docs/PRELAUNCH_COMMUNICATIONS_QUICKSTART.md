## üé§ Spokesperson Preparation Checklist

### üó£Ô∏è Media Interview Preparation

**Before the Interview:**
- [ ] Review key messages and talking points
- [ ] Research journalist/publication (recent articles, style)
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 key points to emphasize
- [ ] Anticipate tough questions and prepare responses
- [ ] Test technology (video/audio) if remote interview
- [ ] Have water and notes ready

**During the Interview:**
- [ ] Start with strong opening statement
- [ ] Bridge to key messages when possible
- [ ] Use concrete examples and data points
- [ ] Stay positive and avoid negative language
- [ ] Keep answers concise (30-60 seconds)
- [ ] Offer to follow up on complex questions

**After the Interview:**
- [ ] Send thank you email within 24 hours
- [ ] Offer additional resources if needed
- [ ] Note any follow-up commitments
- [ ] Share interview link when published
- [ ] Track coverage in media monitoring system

### üö® Crisis Response Protocol

**Immediate Actions:**
1. **Assess**: Quickly determine nature and severity of issue
2. **Notify**: Alert crisis team via predefined communication channel
3. **Contain**: Take immediate steps to limit spread/impact
4. **Investigate**: Gather facts from technical/operational teams
5. **Communicate**: Issue initial holding statement if needed

**Holding Statement Template:**
```
"We are aware of [issue] and are currently investigating. Our team is working diligently to understand the situation and will provide updates as soon as possible. We appreciate your patience and understanding."
```

**Follow-Up Actions:**
- [ ] Determine root cause and resolution timeline
- [ ] Prepare detailed response statement
- [ ] Identify appropriate spokesperson
- [ ] Coordinate with legal/compliance teams
- [ ] Monitor media and social channels
- [ ] Provide regular updates until resolved
