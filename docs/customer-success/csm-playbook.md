# Customer Success Manager (CSM) Playbook

## Overview

This playbook provides Customer Success Managers (CSMs) with day-to-day guidance, best practices, workflows, and resources to ensure customers achieve their business objectives and maximize value from the platform.

---

## CSM Role & Responsibilities

### Primary Responsibilities

1. **Customer Value Realization**
   - Ensure customers achieve their stated success metrics
   - Drive adoption of platform features
   - Identify and deliver quick wins
   - Track and report on ROI

2. **Relationship Management**
   - Build trusted advisor relationships with stakeholders
   - Maintain regular communication cadence
   - Facilitate executive sponsorship
   - Manage stakeholder expectations

3. **Success Plan Execution**
   - Create and maintain customer success plans
   - Track progress against milestones
   - Adjust plans based on changing needs
   - Celebrate wins and learn from setbacks

4. **Health Management**
   - Monitor customer health scores
   - Proactively address red flags
   - Develop and execute recovery plans for at-risk accounts
   - Escalate issues when needed

5. **Advocacy & Growth**
   - Identify expansion opportunities
   - Cultivate customer champions and references
   - Collect testimonials and case studies
   - Facilitate customer advisory board participation

### Secondary Responsibilities

1. **Customer Feedback**
   - Collect product feedback
   - Route requests to appropriate teams
   - Advocate for customer needs internally
   - Validate feature requests

2. **Cross-Functional Collaboration**
   - Work with Sales on upsell opportunities
   - Coordinate with Support on issue resolution
   - Partner with Product on roadmap
   - Align with Marketing on case studies

3. **Administrative**
   - Maintain accurate customer data in CRM
   - Document customer interactions
   - Report on key metrics
   - Participate in team meetings and training

## Daily Routine

### Morning (30-60 minutes)

**Review & Triage (15-20 min)**
- Check email and Slack for urgent customer messages
- Review health score alerts
- Check overnight system status
- Prioritize actions for the day

**Health Score Review (10 min)**
- Review assigned accounts that changed health status
- Flag accounts moving from green to yellow/red
- Note positive improvements to acknowledge

**Communication Planning (10-15 min)**
- Schedule follow-up calls/emails
- Prepare for scheduled meetings
- Review customer context for touchpoints

### Mid-Day (2-4 hours)

**Customer Touchpoints**
- Conduct scheduled customer calls/meetings
- Follow up on previous requests
- Address urgent issues
- Provide proactive value

**Cross-Functional Work**
- Coordinate with Support on tickets
- Discuss expansion with Sales
- Share feedback with Product
- Collaborate with other CSMs

**Administrative Tasks**
- Update CRM records
- Document meeting notes
- Log activities and next steps
- Prepare reports as needed

### Afternoon (30-60 minutes)

**Planning & Preparation (15-20 min)**
- Review next day's calendar
- Prepare for upcoming meetings
- Research customer news/updates
- Plan proactive outreach

**Learning & Development (15-20 min)**
- Review product updates
- Read team communications
- Learn from peer best practices
- Review training materials

**End-of-Day Wrap-up (10 min)**
- Complete any remaining urgent tasks
- Flag items for tomorrow
- Check for late-day customer issues
- Clear inbox

## Key Workflows

### New Customer Onboarding

#### Pre-Onboarding (1 week before kickoff)
1. Receive customer information from Sales
2. Review contract details and tier assignment
3. Research customer industry and business
4. Prepare onboarding checklist
5. Schedule executive kickoff meeting
6. Send pre-kickoff questionnaire to customer

#### Kickoff Week
1. **Executive Kickoff Meeting**
   - Facilitate 90-minute meeting
   - Introduce team and define roles
   - Discuss business objectives
   - Identify success metrics
   - Establish timeline
   - Get executive sponsor commitment

2. **Stakeholder Interviews**
   - Schedule with key stakeholders
   - Document roles and expectations
   - Understand workflows and pain points
   - Identify technical requirements

3. **Success Plan Creation**
   - Consolidate discovery findings
   - Draft success plan
   - Define success metrics and KPIs
   - Establish timeline and milestones
   - Get customer sign-off

#### Technical Setup Phase (Weeks 1-2)
1. Monitor technical progress daily
2. Attend weekly technical syncs
3. Address blockers proactively
4. Keep stakeholders informed
5. Document decisions and changes

#### Training Phase (Weeks 3-4)
1. Coordinate training schedule
2. Attend training sessions when possible
3. Monitor training completion
4. Address training feedback
5. Confirm user readiness

#### Go-Live Phase (Week 5)
1. Conduct go-live meeting
2. Support daily during week 1
3. Monitor adoption closely
4. Address issues immediately
5. Communicate wins to stakeholders

#### Post-Onboarding (Days 31-90)
1. Conduct 30-day business review
2. Conduct 60-day business review
3. Conduct 90-day business review
4. Validate ROI
5. Document success story

### Monthly Business Review

#### Preparation (3-5 days before review)
1. **Gather Data**
   - Pull usage analytics from platform
   - Calculate health score
   - Review success metrics progress
   - Collect feedback from support
   - Check expansion opportunities

2. **Analyze**
   - Identify trends and patterns
   - Calculate progress vs. targets
   - Note areas of concern
   - Identify wins to celebrate
   - Prepare recommendations

3. **Prepare Presentation**
   - Executive summary (1 slide)
   - Success metrics review (2-3 slides)
   - Usage analytics (2-3 slides)
   - Key achievements (1 slide)
   - Areas for improvement (1 slide)
   - Recommendations (1-2 slides)
   - Next steps and action items (1 slide)

4. **Schedule Meeting**
   - Invite key stakeholders
   - Send agenda 2 days in advance
   - Request any customer updates

#### Meeting Execution (45-60 minutes)
1. **Opening (5 min)**
   - Welcome and agenda review
   - Check-ins and updates

2. **Business Review (20-25 min)**
   - Success metrics progress
   - Key achievements and wins
   - Usage analytics and adoption

3. **Discussion (15-20 min)**
   - Areas for improvement
   - Challenges and blockers
   - Recommendations and next steps

4. **Closing (5-10 min)**
   - Action items and owners
   - Next meeting scheduling
   - Thank you and follow-up plan

#### Follow-Up (within 24 hours)
1. Send meeting notes and action items
2. Update success plan
3. Log notes in CRM
4. Schedule next review
5. Execute action items

### At-Risk Account Management

#### Identification
**Red Flags to Watch**:
- Health score drops below 60
- Decreased login frequency
- Increased support tickets
- Negative feedback or complaints
- Leadership changes
- Payment issues
- Renewal risk indicators

#### Assessment (within 24 hours of identification)
1. **Root Cause Analysis**
   - Review recent interactions
   - Check support ticket history
   - Analyze usage patterns
   - Gather internal feedback

2. **Impact Assessment**
   - Determine risk level (yellow vs. red)
   - Identify stakeholders affected
   - Assess business impact
   - Evaluate timeline for resolution

3. **Escalation Decision**
   - Determine if escalation needed
   - Identify appropriate escalation level
   - Prepare escalation documentation

#### Recovery Plan (within 48 hours)
1. **Develop Strategy**
   - Define recovery objectives
   - Identify quick wins
   - Plan stakeholder engagement
   - Set success metrics

2. **Execute Recovery**
   - Schedule executive check-in
   - Address immediate concerns
   - Demonstrate value
   - Rebuild trust

3. **Monitor Progress**
   - Daily check-ins initially
   - Health score monitoring
   - Issue resolution tracking
   - Stakeholder feedback collection

#### Documentation
- Create at-risk account record
- Log all interactions
- Track resolution progress
- Document lessons learned
- Update customer profile

### Expansion Opportunity Management

#### Identification
**Signals**:
- Consistently exceeding usage limits
- New business units or locations
- Requesting features in higher tier
- Growing agent count
- Increasing lead volume
- Positive feedback and satisfaction

#### Qualification
1. **Validate Opportunity**
   - Confirm business need
   - Assess urgency and timeline
   - Identify budget availability
   - Understand decision process

2. **Quantify Value**
   - Calculate potential revenue impact
   - Document ROI case
   - Identify success metrics
   - Prepare business case

#### Engagement
1. **Discovery Meeting**
   - Understand expansion goals
   - Identify key stakeholders
   - Map decision process
   - Establish timeline

2. **Solution Presentation**
   - Present tailored solution
   - Demonstrate value
   - Address concerns
   - Build business case

3. **Proposal & Closing**
   - Collaborate with Sales
   - Present formal proposal
   - Negotiate terms
   - Secure commitment

#### Handoff to Sales
- Document discovery findings
- Share value analysis
- Transfer account context
- Coordinate transition
- Maintain relationship

## Communication Best Practices

### Communication Channels

| Channel | Best For | Response Time | When to Use |
|---------|----------|---------------|-------------|
| Phone | Urgent issues, relationship building | Immediately | Critical issues, important updates |
| Video Call | Meetings, presentations, training | Scheduled | Business reviews, onboarding |
| Email | Formal updates, documentation, follow-ups | < 24 hours | Meeting notes, reports, information |
| Slack/Teams | Quick questions, casual updates | < 2 hours | Quick clarifications, relationship |
| CRM Notes | Internal documentation | N/A | Record keeping, team visibility |

### Communication Principles

1. **Be Proactive, Not Reactive**
   - Reach out before issues escalate
   - Share good news, not just problems
   - Anticipate questions and concerns

2. **Clear and Concise**
   - Get to the point quickly
   - Use bullet points for lists
   - Summarize key takeaways

3. **Value-Focused**
   - Always tie communications to value
   - Use customer's language and metrics
   - Focus on outcomes, not activities

4. **Consistent Cadence**
   - Maintain regular touchpoints
   - Be predictable and reliable
   - Stick to agreed schedules

5. **Multi-Stakeholder Awareness**
   - Keep all relevant parties informed
   - Tailor messaging to audience
   - Facilitate cross-functional alignment

## Health Score Management

### Health Score Components

| Component | Weight | Indicators |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| Engagement (30%) | 0-30 points | Login frequency, feature usage, time in platform |
| Success (30%) | 0-30 points | KPI achievement, goal attainment, time to value |
| Satisfaction (20%) | 0-20 points | CSAT, NPS, ticket satisfaction, complaint count |
| Financial (20%) | 0-20 points | Payment history, renewal intent, expansion revenue |

### Health Score Interpretation

| Score Range | Health Level | Action Required |
|-------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 80-100 | Green (Healthy) | Maintain engagement, monitor for changes |
| 60-79 | Yellow (At Risk) | Increase touchpoints, identify issues, develop action plan |
| 0-59 | Red (Critical) | Escalate to management, execute recovery plan, engage executives |

### Daily Health Score Management

**Green Accounts (80-100)**
- Maintain regular touchpoint cadence
- Look for expansion opportunities
- Ask for referrals and testimonials
- Celebrate wins and successes

**Yellow Accounts (60-79)**
- Increase communication frequency
- Identify root cause of decline
- Develop and execute improvement plan
- Monitor closely (daily/weekly)
- Prepare for potential escalation

**Red Accounts (0-59)**
- Immediate escalation to manager
- Executive engagement
- Daily check-ins
- Recovery plan development
- Document all interactions

## Escalation Procedures

### Escalation Levels

**Level 1: CSM Resolution**
- Issues that CSM can resolve independently
- Standard customer requests
- Minor technical issues
- Timeline: Resolve within 24-48 hours

**Level 2: CSM + Support**
- Issues requiring technical support
- Complex feature requests
- Moderate severity bugs
- Timeline: Resolve within 48-72 hours

**Level 3: CSM Manager Involvement**
- Persistent issues not resolved
- Customer dissatisfaction
- Moderate risk accounts
- Timeline: Resolve within 5 business days

**Level 4: VP Customer Success**
- Critical customer relationships
- High-risk accounts
- Executive-level issues
- Timeline: Resolve within 7 business days

**Level 5: CEO/COO Escalation**
- Strategic partnerships at risk
- Critical enterprise accounts
- Legal or compliance issues
- Timeline: Immediate attention

### Escalation Process

1. **Assess Situation**
   - Determine urgency and impact
   - Identify appropriate escalation level
   - Gather necessary information

2. **Notify Stakeholders**
   - Inform CSM manager of escalation
   - Alert relevant teams (Support, Product, etc.)
   - Notify customer of escalation

3. **Execute Escalation**
   - Follow escalation procedures
   - Coordinate cross-functional response
   - Maintain communication with customer

4. **Resolution**
   - Document resolution steps
   - Update customer on progress
   - Confirm satisfaction with resolution

5. **Post-Escalation Review**
   - Document lessons learned
   - Update processes if needed
   - Close escalation record

## Time Management Tips

### Prioritization Framework

1. **Urgent & Important** (Do First)
   - Critical customer issues
   - Escalations
   - Deadlines today

2. **Important but Not Urgent** (Schedule)
   - Business review preparation
   - Success plan updates
   - Relationship building

3. **Urgent but Not Important** (Delegate or Quick Win)
   - Quick questions
   - Standard requests
   - Minor issues

4. **Neither Urgent nor Important** (Minimize)
   - Long email chains
   - Excessive research
   - Unnecessary meetings

### Time Blocking

- **Morning blocks**: High-focus work (8-11am)
- **Mid-day**: Customer meetings and calls (11am-3pm)
- **Afternoon**: Admin and follow-up (3-5pm)
- **End of day**: Planning and review (4:30-5pm)

### Meeting Efficiency

- Always have an agenda
- Start and end on time
- Take concise notes
- Assign action items with owners and due dates
- Schedule follow-up before ending
