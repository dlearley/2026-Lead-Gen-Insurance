# Sales Methodology & Process

## Overview
Our sales methodology is based on **Consultative Selling**, focusing on understanding the specific challenges of insurance agencies and positioning our lead generation platform as the solution to their growth and efficiency needs.

## The Sales Process

### 1. Prospecting & Discovery
- **Goal**: Identify high-fit prospects and understand their current lead generation and management process.
- **Key Activities**:
    - Outreach via LinkedIn and Email.
    - Initial discovery call (15-20 mins).
    - Qualification using BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline).

### 2. Qualification & Problem Alignment
- **Goal**: Confirm that our platform solves a critical pain point for the prospect.
- **Key Activities**:
    - Deep-dive discovery session.
    - Identification of technical and business requirements.
    - Value proposition alignment.

### 3. Solution Presentation & Demo
- **Goal**: Demonstrate how the platform works and the specific ROI it will deliver.
- **Key Activities**:
    - Tailored product demonstration.
    - Case study walkthrough.
    - ROI calculation.

### 4. Proposal & Negotiation
- **Goal**: Reach an agreement on scope, pricing, and implementation.
- **Key Activities**:
    - Custom proposal delivery.
    - Objection handling.
    - Contract review.

### 5. Closing & Handoff
- **Goal**: Finalize the deal and ensure a smooth transition to Customer Success.
- **Key Activities**:
    - Signature of agreement.
    - Kickoff meeting scheduling.
    - Internal handoff to implementation team.

## Sales Stage Gates

| Stage | Name | Key Criteria to Move to Next Stage |
|-------|------|------------------------------------|
| 1 | Prospecting | Contact made, interest expressed. |
| 2 | Discovery | Pain point identified, BANT qualified. |
| 3 | Demo | Demo completed, technical fit confirmed. |
| 4 | Proposal | Proposal sent, decision maker identified. |
| 5 | Negotiation | Verbal agreement on terms. |
| 6 | Closed Won | Contract signed, payment received. |
| 7 | Closed Lost | Deal lost to competitor or no decision. |
